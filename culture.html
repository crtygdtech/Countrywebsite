The culture of China is a blend of ancient traditions and modern life, heavily influenced by Confucianism, which emphasizes family, hierarchy, and harmony. Includes deep respect for elders and ancestors and the importance of duty to parents. And a strong sense of community and national unity, food is the central to social life and strong focus on family gatherings as well the use of diet therapy for health.

The concept of “face” is crucial since it represents the person’s reputation, dignity, and honor, which individuals are motivated to protect. There is a strong belief in the importance of hard work and education meaning for personal and family advancement, festivals are significant for renewing family bonds and include traditions. China officially recognizes 56 ethnic groups, each with their own unique traditions, languages, and culture, and while mandarin is the most widely spoken, China has hundreds of languages and dialects.
